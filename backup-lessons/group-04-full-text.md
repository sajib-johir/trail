# 91. Abstain

**Pronunciation:** ab-STAYN
**Part of speech:** Verb
**Core meaning:** To choose not to do something.
**Bangla meaning:** বিরত থাকা, নিজেকে সংযত রাখা
**Root story:** From Latin *ab-* = away + *tenere* = to hold. So, **abstain** means “to hold oneself away from something.”
**Simple explanation:** When you abstain, you deliberately avoid an action, habit, vote, food, drink, or behavior.
**Memory hook:** **Abstain = stay away.**
**GRE synonyms:** refrain, desist, forbear, withhold, renounce
**Basic synonyms:** avoid, stop, hold back, not do
**Antonyms:** indulge, participate, continue, consume, engage
**Common usage:** abstain from alcohol, abstain from voting, abstain from comment, abstain from violence
**Confusing word:** Abstain ≠ Obtain. **Abstain** = avoid doing; **obtain** = get something.
**Final memory:** **Abstain = hold yourself away = বিরত থাকা**

## Examples

### Easy Examples

1. He abstained from sugar for a month.
   Meaning: He avoided sugar.
   Bangla: সে চিনি থেকে বিরত ছিল।

2. She abstained from voting.
   Meaning: She chose not to vote.
   Bangla: সে ভোট দেওয়া থেকে বিরত ছিল।

3. I abstain from gossip.
   Meaning: I avoid gossip.
   Bangla: আমি গসিপ থেকে বিরত থাকি।

### GRE / Academic Examples

4. The researcher abstained from making a conclusion until more data were collected.
   Meaning: The researcher avoided a premature conclusion.
   Bangla: যথেষ্ট তথ্যের আগে সিদ্ধান্ত থেকে বিরত থাকলেন।

5. Several committee members abstained because they had conflicts of interest.
   Meaning: They did not vote due to ethical concerns.
   Bangla: স্বার্থের সংঘাত থাকায় তারা ভোট দেননি।

6. A prudent scholar may abstain from speculation when evidence is insufficient.
   Meaning: A careful scholar avoids unsupported claims.
   Bangla: প্রমাণ কম হলে অনুমান থেকে বিরত থাকা।

7. The government urged citizens to abstain from unnecessary travel during the crisis.
   Meaning: People were asked to avoid travel.
   Bangla: অপ্রয়োজনীয় ভ্রমণ থেকে বিরত থাকা।

### Real-World / Paper-Style Examples

   Meaning: They must avoid caffeine to keep results accurate.

    Meaning: Not voting can have different political meanings.

# 94. Corroborate

**Pronunciation:** kuh-ROB-uh-rayt
**Part of speech:** Verb
**Core meaning:** To support a claim with evidence.
**Bangla meaning:** প্রমাণ দ্বারা সমর্থন করা, সত্যতা নিশ্চিত করা

**Simple explanation:** If evidence corroborates a claim, it makes the claim more believable.
**Memory hook:** **Corroborate = cooperate with evidence.**
**GRE synonyms:** confirm, verify, substantiate, validate, authenticate
**Basic synonyms:** prove, support, back up, show

# 120. Vacillate

**Pronunciation:** VAS-uh-layt
**Part of speech:** Verb
**Core meaning:** To keep changing your mind; to waver between choices.
**Bangla meaning:** দোদুল্যমান হওয়া, সিদ্ধান্তহীনতায় ভোগা, এদিক-ওদিক মত বদলানো
**Root story:** From Latin *vacillare*, meaning “to sway, waver, stagger.”
**Simple explanation:** When you vacillate, you cannot decide firmly between options.
**Memory hook:** **Vacillate = swing like a fan between choices.**
**GRE synonyms:** waver, fluctuate, oscillate, hesitate, equivocate
**Basic synonyms:** be unsure, change mind, go back and forth
**Antonyms:** decide, resolve, determine, commit, persevere
**Common usage:** vacillate between options, vacillate over a decision, vacillating leader, vacillating policy
**Confusing word:** Vacillate ≠ Vaccinate. **Vacillate** = hesitate/change mind; **vaccinate** = give vaccine.
**Final memory:** **Vacillate = keep wavering between choices = দোদুল্যমান হওয়া**

# Day 4 Revision Section

## High-Priority Confusing Pairs

**Enervate vs Invigorate**
Enervate = weaken.
Invigorate = energize.

**Obsolete vs Opaque**
Obsolete = no longer used.
Opaque = unclear or not transparent.

**Pedantic vs Punctilious**
Pedantic = annoyingly detail-focused.
Punctilious = carefully attentive to detail/rules, often positive or neutral.

**Placid vs Tranquil**
Placid = calm, not easily disturbed.
Tranquil = peaceful and undisturbed.

**Prophetic vs Prescient**
Prophetic = seeming to predict the future.
Prescient = having foresight.

**Precipitate vs Prudent**
Precipitate = rash or sudden.
Prudent = careful and wise.

**Scrupulous vs Unscrupulous**
Scrupulous = ethical and careful.
Unscrupulous = dishonest and immoral.

**Vacillate vs Oscillate**
Vacillate = mentally waver between choices.
Oscillate = move back and forth physically or figuratively.
