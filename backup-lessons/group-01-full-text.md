## 1. Abound

**Pronunciation:** ə-BOUND
**Part of speech:** Verb
**Core meaning:** To exist in large numbers or large quantity.
**Bangla meaning:** প্রচুর পরিমাণে থাকা, ভরপুর থাকা
**Root story:** From Latin *abundare*, meaning “to overflow.” Imagine water overflowing from a river.
**Simple explanation:** If something abounds, there is a lot of it.
**Memory hook:** **Abound = abundance everywhere.**
**GRE synonyms:** teem, proliferate, swarm, flourish, overflow
**Basic synonyms:** be plentiful, be full of, exist widely, be common
**Antonyms:** lack, be scarce, be rare, disappear
**Common usage:** opportunities abound, evidence abounds, wildlife abounds, rumors abound
**Confusing word:** **Abound ≠ Abandon**. Abound = প্রচুর থাকা; abandon = ছেড়ে দেওয়া।
**Final memory:** **Abound = overflow with quantity = প্রচুর থাকা**

### 10 Examples

1. Fish abound in this river.
2. Opportunities abound in large cities.
3. Flowers abound in spring.
4. Evidence abounds that regular exercise improves health.
5. Errors abound in the poorly edited report.
6. Renewable energy resources abound in sunny coastal regions.
7. In complex engineering projects, constraints abound.
8. Rumors abounded after the sudden resignation of the minister.
9. In biodiversity studies, insects often abound in humid forests.
10. Although data abound, clear interpretation remains difficult.

### GRE Synonym Examples — 15 Sentences

1. Mosquitoes **teem** in stagnant water after heavy rain.
2. The forest **teems** with wildlife.
3. The report **teems** with statistical errors.
4. The city **teemed** with visitors during the festival.
5. The old archive **teems** with forgotten letters.
6. Online rumors can **proliferate** rapidly after a political crisis.
7. Small startups began to **proliferate** after the new tax policy.
8. Misinformation may **proliferate** when official communication is weak.
9. Factories **proliferated** near the port after trade expanded.
10. Research papers on artificial intelligence continue to **proliferate**.
11. Reporters **swarmed** around the official after the announcement.
12. Complaints **swarmed** the company’s website after the product failed.
13. Supporters **swarmed** the stadium after the victory.
14. The market **swarmed** with cheap imitation products.
15. Questions **swarmed** in his mind before the interview.

## 2. Amorphous

**Pronunciation:** ə-MOR-fəs
**Part of speech:** Adjective
**Core meaning:** Having no clear shape, form, or structure.
**Bangla meaning:** নির্দিষ্ট আকারহীন, অস্পষ্ট গঠনের
**Root story:** Greek *a-* = without + *morphē* = form. So, **amorphous = without form**.
**Simple explanation:** Something amorphous has no fixed shape or clear organization.
**Memory hook:** **A + morph = no form.**
**GRE synonyms:** nebulous, vague, indeterminate, formless, shapeless
**Basic synonyms:** unclear, loose, undefined, unstructured
**Antonyms:** structured, definite, clear, organized, well-formed
**Common usage:** amorphous idea, amorphous mass, amorphous plan, amorphous concept
**Confusing word:** **Amorphous ≠ Ambiguous**. Amorphous = shape/structure unclear; ambiguous = meaning unclear.
**Final memory:** **Amorphous = without clear form = আকারহীন / অস্পষ্ট**

### 10 Examples

1. The cloud looked amorphous.
2. The jelly became an amorphous mass.
3. His drawing was amorphous and hard to identify.
4. The proposal was criticized for being amorphous.
5. His argument remained amorphous and difficult to evaluate.
6. The committee rejected the amorphous plan.
7. The company had an amorphous management structure.
8. In materials science, amorphous solids lack a regular crystal structure.
9. In policy writing, an amorphous goal is hard to measure.
10. The theory sounded impressive but remained too amorphous to test.

### GRE Synonym Examples — 15 Sentences

1. The politician gave a **nebulous** answer to avoid responsibility.
2. Her career goals were still **nebulous** after graduation.
3. The contract contained **nebulous** terms that confused both parties.
4. The committee disliked the **nebulous** proposal.
5. The concept of fairness can become **nebulous** in complex legal cases.
6. His explanation was too **vague** to be useful.
7. The witness gave a **vague** description of the thief.
8. The research question remained **vague** and unfocused.
9. The instructions were **vague**, so the students made different mistakes.
10. The manager’s **vague** promises failed to reassure employees.
11. The result was **indeterminate** because the data were incomplete.
12. The experiment produced an **indeterminate** outcome.
13. His answer was **indeterminate**, neither yes nor no.
14. The disease had **indeterminate** symptoms at first.
15. The legal status of the document remained **indeterminate**.

## 3. Austere

**Bangla meaning:** কঠোর, সাদাসিধে, আড়ম্বরহীন

**Simple explanation:** Austere means simple and strict, often with little comfort or decoration.

**Basic synonyms:** strict, plain, simple, harsh
**Antonyms:** luxurious, indulgent, ornate, soft, lavish
**Common usage:** austere lifestyle, austere room, austere policy, austere discipline
**Confusing word:** **Austere ≠ Aesthetic**. Austere = strict/plain; aesthetic = related to beauty.
**Final memory:** **Austere = strict + plain = কঠোর ও সাদাসিধে**

## 4. Belie

**Pronunciation:** bih-LY
**Part of speech:** Verb
**Core meaning:** To give a false impression; to contradict the truth.
**Bangla meaning:** মিথ্যা ধারণা দেওয়া, আসল সত্যকে ভুলভাবে দেখানো
**Root story:** From Old English *beleogan*, meaning “to deceive by lies.”
**Simple explanation:** If something belies the truth, it makes the truth look different from reality.
**Memory hook:** **Belie has “lie” inside = false impression.**
**GRE synonyms:** contradict, negate, disprove, misrepresent, mask
**Basic synonyms:** hide, conceal, disguise, distort
**Antonyms:** reveal, confirm, show, prove, expose
**Common usage:** smile belies sadness, calmness belies fear, appearance belies strength
**Confusing word:** **Belie ≠ Believe**. Belie = false impression; believe = trust.
**Final memory:** **Belie = make the truth look false = মিথ্যা ধারণা দেওয়া**

### 10 Examples

1. Her smile belied her sadness.
2. His calm voice belied his fear.
3. The old house belied its strong structure.
4. The company’s confident statement belied its financial problems.
5. His modest appearance belied his great influence.
6. The peaceful surface belied the political tension beneath.
7. The simple graph belied the complexity of the data.
8. Mild symptoms may belie a serious disease.
9. Smooth machine operation may belie hidden mechanical fatigue.
10. National averages can belie regional inequality.

### GRE Synonym Examples — 15 Sentences

1. The evidence **contradicted** the witness’s testimony.
2. His actions **contradicted** his promises.
3. The new data **contradict** the earlier theory.
4. Her cheerful tone **contradicted** the seriousness of the news.
5. The company’s losses **contradicted** its claims of success.
6. The result **negated** the researcher’s original assumption.
7. His confession **negated** the need for further speculation.
8. The discovery **negated** years of accepted belief.
9. The contract clause **negated** the verbal promise.
10. One clear fact can **negate** a weak argument.
11. The DNA evidence **disproved** the accusation.

13. The figures **disproved** the minister’s claim.
14. His calm appearance **masked** deep anxiety.
15. The official report **misrepresented** the actual conditions.
