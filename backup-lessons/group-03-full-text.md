# 61. Abate

**Pronunciation:** ə-BAYT
**Part of speech:** Verb
**Core meaning:** To become less strong, less intense, or smaller in amount.
**Bangla meaning:** কমে যাওয়া, হ্রাস পাওয়া, প্রশমিত হওয়া
**Root story:** From Old French/Latin idea of “beating down” or “reducing.” Imagine a storm being “beaten down” until its force becomes weaker.
**Simple explanation:** If pain, anger, rain, wind, noise, fear, or tension abates, it becomes less intense.
**Memory hook:** **Abate = a bad thing becomes less.**
**GRE synonyms:** subside, lessen, diminish, decrease, wane, ebb
**Basic synonyms:** reduce, calm down, go down, become weaker
**Antonyms:** intensify, increase, escalate, worsen, amplify
**Common usage:** storm abated, pain abated, fear abated, tension abated, pollution abatement
**Confusing word:** **Abate ≠ Abet.** Abate = কমে যাওয়া; abet = অপরাধে সাহায্য করা।

## Examples

**Easy examples**

1. The rain finally abated.
   Meaning: The rain became weaker.
   Bangla: বৃষ্টি কমে গেল।

2. His anger abated after he heard the truth.
   Meaning: His anger became less strong.
   Bangla: রাগ কমে গেল।

3. The pain abated after taking medicine.
   Meaning: The pain decreased.
   Bangla: ব্যথা কমে গেল।

**GRE / Academic examples**

4. Public anxiety abated once the government released clear data.
   Meaning: Fear became less intense after information was provided.
   Bangla: জনভয় কমে গেল।

5. The economic pressure did not abate despite temporary policy changes.
   Meaning: The pressure remained strong.
   Bangla: অর্থনৈতিক চাপ কমেনি।

6. Tensions between the two groups abated after several rounds of negotiation.
   Meaning: Conflict became less intense.
   Bangla: উত্তেজনা কমে গেল।

7. The scientist waited for background noise to abate before recording the signal.
   Meaning: The noise needed to decrease.
   Bangla: শব্দ কমা দরকার ছিল।

**Real-world / paper-style examples**

8. In environmental reports, pollution may abate after strict emission controls are enforced.
   Meaning: Pollution can decrease because of regulation.
   Bangla: দূষণ কমে।

9. In medical writing, symptoms may abate temporarily without curing the disease.
   Meaning: Symptoms can become weaker for a while.
   Bangla: উপসর্গ সাময়িকভাবে কমতে পারে।

10. In conflict studies, violence often abates when communication channels reopen.
    Meaning: Violence decreases when dialogue begins.
    Bangla: যোগাযোগ শুরু হলে সহিংসতা কমে।

## GRE synonym examples

1. The protests began to **subside** after the mayor promised reforms.
2. The patient’s fever **lessened** overnight.
3. The influence of the old theory gradually **diminished**.
4. Investor panic began to **decrease** after the central bank intervened.
5. Public enthusiasm **waned** when the project faced delays.
6. The floodwaters slowly **ebbed** after the rain stopped.
7. The committee hoped the controversy would **subside** before the election.
8. The intensity of the debate **lessened** after new evidence appeared.
9. The researcher found that stress levels **diminished** after meditation.
10. Demand for the product **decreased** during the recession.
11. His confidence **waned** after repeated failures.
12. Political hostility **ebbed** after the peace agreement.
13. The storm’s force **subsided** by morning.
14. The drug helped **lessen** inflammation.
15. The threat did not **diminish** as quickly as officials had expected.

**Final memory line:** **Abate = intensity goes down = কমে যাওয়া**

# 62. Abjure

**Pronunciation:** ab-JOOR
**Part of speech:** Verb
**Core meaning:** To formally reject, renounce, or give up a belief, claim, habit, or loyalty.
**Bangla meaning:** শপথ করে ত্যাগ করা, পরিত্যাগ করা, অস্বীকার করা
**Root story:** From Latin **ab-** = away + **jurare** = to swear. So abjure means “to swear away” from something.
**Simple explanation:** To abjure something means to reject it seriously and publicly, often after once believing in it.
**Memory hook:** **Abjure = swear to reject.**
**GRE synonyms:** renounce, repudiate, reject, forswear, recant, disavow
**Basic synonyms:** give up, leave, deny, abandon
**Antonyms:** embrace, accept, affirm, support, maintain
**Common usage:** abjure violence, abjure old beliefs, abjure former loyalty, abjure a claim
**Confusing word:** **Abjure ≠ Adjure.** Abjure = formally reject; adjure = solemnly urge or command.

## Examples

**Easy examples**

1. He abjured smoking after becoming ill.
   Meaning: He gave up smoking seriously.
   Bangla: ধূমপান ত্যাগ করল।

2. She abjured her old habits.
   Meaning: She rejected her old behavior.
   Bangla: পুরোনো অভ্যাস ত্যাগ করল।

3. The leader abjured violence.
   Meaning: The leader rejected violence.
   Bangla: সহিংসতা পরিত্যাগ করলেন।

**GRE / Academic examples**

4. The politician abjured his earlier extremist views.
   Meaning: He formally rejected views he once held.
   Bangla: আগের চরমপন্থী মত ত্যাগ করলেন।

5. The reformer urged the group to abjure corruption and embrace transparency.
   Meaning: The group was asked to reject corruption.
   Bangla: দুর্নীতি ত্যাগ করা।

6. After the scandal, the organization abjured all links with the controversial donor.
   Meaning: It rejected association with the donor.
   Bangla: সম্পর্ক অস্বীকার করল।

7. The philosopher did not merely question luxury; he abjured it entirely.
   Meaning: He completely rejected luxury.
   Bangla: বিলাসিতা পুরোপুরি ত্যাগ করলেন।

**Real-world / paper-style examples**

8. In political transitions, former militants may be required to abjure violence before joining negotiations.
   Meaning: They must formally reject violence.
   Bangla: সহিংসতা পরিত্যাগ করতে হয়।

9. In ethical research, scientists must abjure data manipulation.
   Meaning: They must reject dishonest practices.
   Bangla: তথ্য বিকৃতি ত্যাগ।

10. In religious history, converts often abjured former doctrines before adopting a new faith.
    Meaning: They formally rejected old beliefs.
    Bangla: পুরোনো মতবাদ ত্যাগ।

## GRE synonym examples

1. The king was forced to **renounce** his claim to the throne.
2. The scholar **repudiated** the theory after new evidence emerged.
3. The committee **rejected** the proposal as impractical.
4. The witness agreed to **forswear** any false testimony.
5. The accused scientist **recanted** his earlier statement.
6. The party **disavowed** the candidate’s inflammatory remarks.
7. The monk **renounced** material comfort.
8. The government **repudiated** the illegal treaty.
9. The editor **rejected** the article for weak evidence.
10. The reformer **forswore** personal privilege.
11. Under pressure, the activist refused to **recant** his criticism.
12. The company **disavowed** responsibility for the unsafe product.
13. She **renounced** revenge and chose reconciliation.
14. The university **repudiated** discriminatory policies.
15. He **rejected** the claim that success required dishonesty.

**Final memory line:** **Abjure = swear away from something = শপথ করে ত্যাগ করা**

# 63. Anomalous

**Pronunciation:** ə-NOM-ə-ləs
**Part of speech:** Adjective
**Core meaning:** Different from what is normal, expected, or usual.
**Bangla meaning:** অস্বাভাবিক, ব্যতিক্রমী, অনিয়মিত
**Root story:** From Greek idea **an-** = not + **homalos** = even or regular. So anomalous means “not regular.”
**Simple explanation:** Something anomalous does not fit the normal pattern. In GRE, it often means unusual data, strange behavior, or unexpected results.
**Memory hook:** **Anomalous = anomaly-like = not normal.**
**GRE synonyms:** abnormal, irregular, atypical, aberrant, deviant
**Basic synonyms:** unusual, strange, unexpected, odd
**Antonyms:** normal, typical, regular, standard, conventional
**Common usage:** anomalous result, anomalous data, anomalous behavior, anomalous pattern
**Confusing word:** **Anomalous ≠ Anonymous.** Anomalous = abnormal; anonymous = unnamed.

## Examples

**Easy examples**

1. The result was anomalous.
   Meaning: The result was unusual.
   Bangla: ফলটি অস্বাভাবিক ছিল।

2. His behavior seemed anomalous today.
   Meaning: He acted differently from usual.
   Bangla: আচরণ অস্বাভাবিক।

3. The weather was anomalous for winter.
   Meaning: The weather was not typical.
   Bangla: শীতের জন্য অস্বাভাবিক।

**GRE / Academic examples**

4. The researcher repeated the experiment to check whether the anomalous result was an error.
   Meaning: The result did not match the expected pattern.
   Bangla: অস্বাভাবিক ফল।

5. The data set contained one anomalous value that distorted the average.
   Meaning: One unusual value affected the result.
   Bangla: ব্যতিক্রমী মান।

6. The committee viewed the sudden increase as anomalous rather than representative.
   Meaning: The increase was unusual, not normal.
   Bangla: সাধারণ প্রবণতা নয়।

7. The patient’s anomalous symptoms made diagnosis difficult.
   Meaning: The symptoms did not follow the usual pattern.
   Bangla: অস্বাভাবিক উপসর্গ।

**Real-world / paper-style examples**

8. In climate science, anomalous temperature readings may indicate measurement error or genuine environmental change.
   Meaning: Unusual readings need careful analysis.
   Bangla: অস্বাভাবিক তাপমাত্রা।

9. In engineering tests, anomalous vibration signals can reveal hidden defects.
   Meaning: Strange signals may show a problem.
   Bangla: অস্বাভাবিক কম্পন।

10. In financial reports, anomalous transactions are often examined for fraud risk.
    Meaning: Unusual transactions may be suspicious.
    Bangla: অস্বাভাবিক লেনদেন।

## GRE synonym examples

1. The patient showed **abnormal** immune responses.
2. The report identified **irregular** voting patterns.
3. The sample was **atypical** of the wider population.
4. The machine produced **aberrant** readings during the test.
5. The behavior was considered **deviant** by social standards.
6. The sensor detected an **abnormal** rise in pressure.
7. The accountant noticed **irregular** entries in the ledger.
8. The student’s performance was **atypical**, given his earlier scores.
9. The study excluded **aberrant** data points.
10. The policy treated **deviant** cases separately.
11. The organism developed an **abnormal** growth pattern.
12. The rainfall pattern was **irregular** throughout the season.
13. His reaction was **atypical** for such a calm person.
14. The telescope recorded an **aberrant** signal.
15. The model failed because of several **irregular** observations.

**Final memory line:** **Anomalous = outside the normal pattern = অস্বাভাবিক**

# 64. Antipathy

**Pronunciation:** an-TIP-ə-thee
**Part of speech:** Noun
**Core meaning:** A strong feeling of dislike, hostility, or opposition.
**Bangla meaning:** তীব্র বিরাগ, ঘৃণা, বিদ্বেষ, অপছন্দ
**Root story:** From Greek **anti-** = against + **pathos** = feeling. Antipathy literally means “feeling against.”
**Simple explanation:** Antipathy is stronger than simple dislike. It suggests deep emotional opposition.
**Memory hook:** **Anti + pathy = feeling against someone/something.**
**GRE synonyms:** aversion, hostility, animosity, enmity, antagonism
**Basic synonyms:** dislike, hatred, opposition, resentment
**Antonyms:** sympathy, affection, fondness, liking, goodwill
**Common usage:** deep antipathy, personal antipathy, political antipathy, antipathy toward authority
**Confusing word:** **Antipathy ≠ Apathy.** Antipathy = strong dislike; apathy = no feeling or interest.

## Examples

**Easy examples**

1. He felt antipathy toward liars.
   Meaning: He strongly disliked liars.
   Bangla: মিথ্যাবাদীদের প্রতি বিরাগ।

2. Her antipathy was obvious.
   Meaning: Her dislike was clear.
   Bangla: ঘৃণা স্পষ্ট ছিল।

3. The two neighbors had mutual antipathy.
   Meaning: They disliked each other.
   Bangla: পারস্পরিক বিরাগ।

**GRE / Academic examples**

4. The senator’s antipathy toward regulation shaped his economic policy.
   Meaning: His strong dislike of regulation influenced his decisions.
   Bangla: নিয়ন্ত্রণের প্রতি বিরাগ।

5. The critic’s antipathy to modern art made his review predictable.
   Meaning: His strong dislike affected his judgment.
   Bangla: আধুনিক শিল্পের প্রতি অপছন্দ।

6. Years of rivalry created deep antipathy between the two institutions.
   Meaning: Long conflict produced hostility.
   Bangla: গভীর বিদ্বেষ।

7. The reform failed partly because of public antipathy toward higher taxes.
   Meaning: People strongly opposed higher taxes.
   Bangla: কর বৃদ্ধির প্রতি বিরাগ।

**Real-world / paper-style examples**

8. In political analysis, antipathy toward elites can shape voting behavior.
   Meaning: Strong dislike can influence political choices.
   Bangla: অভিজাতদের প্রতি বিরাগ।

9. In organizational studies, employee antipathy toward management may reduce cooperation.
   Meaning: Hostility harms teamwork.
   Bangla: ব্যবস্থাপনার প্রতি বিরাগ।

10. In social psychology, antipathy can develop from repeated negative contact between groups.
    Meaning: Repeated bad experience can create hostility.
    Bangla: বারবার খারাপ অভিজ্ঞতায় বিরাগ তৈরি হয়।

## GRE synonym examples

1. She had an **aversion** to public praise.
2. The negotiations failed because of mutual **hostility**.
3. His **animosity** toward the rival party was obvious.
4. The long war created deep **enmity** between the nations.
5. The policy generated strong **antagonism** among workers.
6. The patient had an **aversion** to bitter medicine.
7. The speech increased **hostility** rather than reducing it.
8. Personal **animosity** clouded his judgment.
9. The tribes’ historical **enmity** complicated peace talks.
10. Her reforms faced **antagonism** from conservative officials.
11. He expressed an **aversion** to unnecessary risk.
12. Public **hostility** grew after the scandal.
13. The article revealed the author’s **animosity** toward industrialization.
14. Centuries of **enmity** could not be erased quickly.
15. The proposal met fierce **antagonism** from local residents.

**Final memory line:** **Antipathy = feeling against = তীব্র বিরাগ**

# 65. Arcane

**Pronunciation:** ar-KAYN
**Part of speech:** Adjective
**Core meaning:** Secret, mysterious, or understood by only a few people.
**Bangla meaning:** গুপ্ত, রহস্যময়, দুর্বোধ্য, অল্প কয়েকজনের জানা
**Root story:** From Latin **arcanus**, meaning secret or hidden, related to **arca**, a chest or box. Imagine knowledge locked inside a hidden chest.
**Simple explanation:** Arcane knowledge is not common knowledge. It is specialized, mysterious, or difficult for ordinary people to understand.
**Memory hook:** **Arcane = knowledge locked in a secret box.**
**GRE synonyms:** esoteric, obscure, mysterious, cryptic, abstruse
**Basic synonyms:** secret, hidden, difficult, specialized
**Antonyms:** common, obvious, familiar, accessible, clear
**Common usage:** arcane rules, arcane rituals, arcane knowledge, arcane legal language
**Confusing word:** **Arcane ≠ Archaic.** Arcane = mysterious/secret; archaic = old-fashioned.

## Examples

**Easy examples**

1. The book was full of arcane symbols.
   Meaning: The symbols were mysterious.
   Bangla: রহস্যময় চিহ্ন।

2. He studied arcane traditions.
   Meaning: He studied secret or little-known traditions.
   Bangla: গুপ্ত ঐতিহ্য।

3. The rule seemed arcane to beginners.
   Meaning: Beginners could not understand it easily.
   Bangla: নতুনদের কাছে দুর্বোধ্য।

**GRE / Academic examples**

4. The professor explained an arcane theory in simple language.
   Meaning: The theory was difficult and specialized.
   Bangla: জটিল তত্ত্ব।

5. The legal document contained arcane terminology that confused the public.
   Meaning: The terms were difficult and specialized.
   Bangla: দুর্বোধ্য আইনগত শব্দ।

6. The committee rejected the proposal because it depended on arcane technical assumptions.
   Meaning: The assumptions were too specialized and hard to verify.
   Bangla: বিশেষজ্ঞ ছাড়া বোঝা কঠিন।

7. What appeared arcane to outsiders was routine knowledge among specialists.
   Meaning: It was mysterious only to non-experts.
   Bangla: অ-বিশেষজ্ঞদের কাছে রহস্যময়।

**Real-world / paper-style examples**

8. In computer security, arcane details of encryption can determine system safety.
   Meaning: Specialized hidden details matter.
   Bangla: এনক্রিপশনের জটিল বিষয়।

9. In historical research, scholars often decode arcane manuscripts to understand lost traditions.
   Meaning: Old specialized texts require expert interpretation.
   Bangla: গুপ্ত পাণ্ডুলিপি।

10. In tax law, arcane regulations can make compliance difficult for small businesses.
    Meaning: Complex rules are hard to follow.
    Bangla: দুর্বোধ্য করনীতি।

## GRE synonym examples

1. The ritual was too **esoteric** for outsiders to understand.
2. The poem’s meaning remained **obscure**.
3. The cave painting had a **mysterious** purpose.
4. His reply was **cryptic**, leaving everyone confused.
5. The philosopher used **abstruse** arguments.
6. The lecture on quantum fields was **esoteric**.
7. The origin of the custom is **obscure**.
8. The scientist investigated a **mysterious** signal.
9. The note was so **cryptic** that nobody knew what to do.
10. The article was **abstruse** even for graduate students.
11. The group preserved **esoteric** teachings.
12. The historian found **obscure** references in old archives.
13. The cause of the disease remained **mysterious**.
14. His **cryptic** warning became clear only later.
15. The manual was filled with **abstruse** formulas.

**Final memory line:** **Arcane = hidden specialist knowledge = গুপ্ত / দুর্বোধ্য**

# 66. Arduous

**Pronunciation:** AR-joo-əs
**Part of speech:** Adjective
**Core meaning:** Very difficult and requiring great effort.
**Bangla meaning:** কঠিন, পরিশ্রমসাধ্য, শ্রমসাধ্য
**Root story:** From Latin **arduus**, meaning high, steep, or difficult. Imagine climbing a steep mountain.
**Simple explanation:** An arduous task is not just difficult; it requires long, serious effort.
**Memory hook:** **Arduous = hard uphill climb.**
**GRE synonyms:** strenuous, laborious, onerous, taxing, demanding
**Basic synonyms:** hard, difficult, tiring, challenging
**Antonyms:** easy, effortless, simple, undemanding
**Common usage:** arduous journey, arduous task, arduous training, arduous process
**Confusing word:** **Arduous ≠ Ardent.** Arduous = difficult; ardent = passionate or enthusiastic.

## Examples

**Easy examples**

1. The hike was arduous.
   Meaning: The hike was very difficult.
   Bangla: হাঁটাটি কঠিন ছিল।

2. Preparing for the exam was arduous.
   Meaning: It required much effort.
   Bangla: পরীক্ষার প্রস্তুতি শ্রমসাধ্য।

3. The road to the village was arduous.
   Meaning: The road was hard to travel.
   Bangla: পথটি কষ্টকর।

**GRE / Academic examples**

4. The researchers faced the arduous task of collecting data from remote regions.
   Meaning: The task required great effort.
   Bangla: কঠিন তথ্য সংগ্রহ।

5. The reform process was arduous because many institutions resisted change.
   Meaning: Reform was difficult and slow.
   Bangla: সংস্কার প্রক্রিয়া কঠিন ছিল।

6. Writing a credible literature review can be arduous for new researchers.
   Meaning: It needs patience and effort.
   Bangla: সাহিত্য পর্যালোচনা শ্রমসাধ্য।

7. The company survived an arduous restructuring after the financial crisis.
   Meaning: The restructuring was difficult and demanding.
   Bangla: কঠিন পুনর্গঠন।

**Real-world / paper-style examples**

8. In engineering design, validating a model under multiple conditions is often arduous.
   Meaning: Testing across conditions takes serious effort.
   Bangla: মডেল যাচাই কঠিন।

9. In public health, vaccine distribution in remote areas can be arduous.
   Meaning: Logistics are difficult.
   Bangla: দূরবর্তী এলাকায় টিকা বিতরণ কঠিন।

10. In field research, arduous terrain can limit data collection.
    Meaning: Difficult land conditions slow research.
    Bangla: কঠিন ভূখণ্ড গবেষণা বাধাগ্রস্ত করে।

6. The athlete followed a **strenuous** training schedule.
7. The historian completed a **laborious** review of old documents.

9. The debate was emotionally **taxing** for both sides.
10. The job is **demanding** but rewarding.
11. The mountain route was **strenuous**.
12. Cleaning the corrupted data was **laborious**.
13. The loan terms were **onerous** for poor borrowers.
14. Long simulations can be computationally **taxing**.
15. The professor assigned a **demanding** research paper.

**Final memory line:** **Arduous = hard + effortful = পরিশ্রমসাধ্য**

# 67. Artless

**Pronunciation:** ART-ləs
**Part of speech:** Adjective
**Core meaning:** Natural, sincere, and without deception; sometimes simple or naive.
**Bangla meaning:** সরল, অকপট, নিষ্পাপ, কপটতাহীন
**Root story:** Artless means “without artifice.” Here, “art” means clever trick or artificial skill. So artless = without trickery.
**Simple explanation:** An artless person speaks or behaves simply and honestly, without trying to manipulate others.
**Memory hook:** **Artless = no artificial trick = sincere.**
**GRE synonyms:** guileless, naive, ingenuous, unaffected, innocent
**Basic synonyms:** simple, honest, natural, sincere
**Antonyms:** artful, cunning, crafty, deceitful, calculating
**Common usage:** artless charm, artless honesty, artless smile, artless innocence
**Confusing word:** **Artless ≠ Artful.** Artless = sincere/simple; artful = clever, crafty, sometimes deceptive.

## Examples

**Easy examples**

# 74. Canonize

# 76. Chicanery

**Part of speech:** Noun

**Bangla meaning:** ছলনা, প্রতারণা, কৌশলী ধোঁকাবাজি

**Simple explanation:** Chicanery is not a simple lie only. It is clever, manipulative dishonesty, often in politics, business, law, or negotiation.

**Confusing word:** **Chicanery ≠ Chic.** Chic = stylish; chicanery = dishonest trickery.

Ascetic = self-denying; aesthetic = related to beauty.

**Censure ≠ Censor**
Censure = criticize; censor = suppress information.
